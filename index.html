about

Systems all the way down.

I'm a Computer Engineering undergraduate with a deep interest in how computing systems actually work — not just how to use them. That means spending time in the layers most people prefer to abstract away: instruction sets, memory hierarchies, bare-metal firmware and the hardware that runs it all.

I'm drawn to systems programming — writing software that talks directly to hardware, has real constraints, and where a wrong pointer means a crash rather than a stack trace. C, C++ and Rust feel more honest to me than most things that run above them.

Outside of that I work on hardware design, RISC-V microarchitecture, robotics software and applied math problems that show up when you get close enough to physical systems.
